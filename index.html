About Me


Hey there! As you've probably noticed by now, I'm Sam. Born and raised in Miami Beach by two parents in the technology industry, I grew up with computers as practically another sibling. From as early as nine years old, I've been designing t-shirts and miscellaneous graphics. Throughout high school and my tenure at the University of Miami, I have continued to cultivate these skills, mostly through freelance jobs designing logos, and app and website mockups. When I'm not designing user interfaces, you can most likely find me playing quidditch (yes, it's a real sport) or on a Netflix binge. In the future, I'd love to move to the West Coast to continue developing my design abilities. If you have any questions, don't hesitate to contact me.

Experience

Responsive Architecture and Design Lab

Using existing technology to drag the architecture industry into the 21st century, I am personally responsible for providing code for the rapid prototypes we develop in the lab. Past projects have included developing an instant replay system for the sport of fencing using the Microsoft Kinect and Processing, an Alage Bio-Reactor where the intensity of the algae in each jar was controlled by Arduino powered LEDS, and an augemented reality app of our office using Unity and Google's Project Tango.

University of Miami Quidditch

I have been the Graphic Design and Merchandise Coordinator for the University of Miami Quidditch team since September 2013. I design and order team logo, shirts, jerseys, and other merchandise. I was also the Public Relations Chair for the 2013-2014 school year. Under this position, I would manage the social media sites that represent the club, including Facebook, Twitter, and Tumblr in order to share club information and events with the University of Miami community.

MakeSchool

During the summer of 2014, I learned Objective-C to design and code an iPhone game from the ground up and demoed this game in front of major companies such as Apple, Facebook, Electronic Arts, GameLoft. I adapted the leading industry techniques for product design, prototyping, monetization, marketing, analytics to use in my own game.
